Welcome to robo i/o!

Robo i/o is a two day workshop for junior high schoolers hosted by OHI/O, Ohio State's hackathon program. OHI/O aims to foster a tech culture at Ohio State and its surrounding communities.

We aim to provide junior high school students an opportunity to learn about computer science in a fun, engaging, and inclusive environment. Students will work in teams to build projects with TI-Nspire calculators and innvator hubs. Students will also gain the opportunity to develop new friendships, build connections with professional mentors, and learn soft skills that will help them succeed in the real world.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Robo I/O cost money?

Robo I/O is free to participate in thanks to the Ohio State and our supporters.

What should I bring?

Be sure to dress comfortably, and bring anything you might need during the day.

Where is it and how do I get there?

Enarson Classroom Building
2009 Millikin Rd
Columbus, OH 43210

What will I eat?

We will be providing lunch and snacks for all participants. We've got participant's food restrictions covered too.

Can my parents come with me? Can I stay with my student?

Parents are more than welcome to visit at any time during this event. We encourage parents to attend the showcase Sunday at 3:00 PM to check out all of the amazing work that the students have created.

How do teams work? Do I need to have a team?

You will get to work in small teams of 4 students. Teams will be assigned and there will be ice breakers the morning of.
